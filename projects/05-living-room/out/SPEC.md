## 1. Project narrative

A real-estate staging-style presentation for a coastal-California living
room — the kind of room a buyer should walk into and immediately picture
themselves on the sectional with a glass of wine. The space reads
contemporary but warm: a neutral envelope of white walls and a coffered
ceiling lets the wood floor, walnut accent furniture, and indigo rug do the
heavy emotional lifting.

**Design intent**

- **Lounge first, work second.** The L-shape sectional anchors the south
  wall; two facing lounge chairs across the coffee table create a
  conversation triangle that doesn't aim at a TV.
- **Reflect light, don't block it.** A 4'-0" × 5'-0" operable casement on
  the east wall is the room's primary daylight source. Sheer linen drapery
  diffuses afternoon light without darkening the space; the 4'-0" × 5'-0"
  mirror over the console on the west wall bounces that light back across
  the room.
- **A coffered ceiling, lit gently.** A 4×4 grid of 36" × 42" coffers
  hosts 16 recessed downlights — wash, not spotlights — at 2700K so the
  room reads warm at night.
- **Wide opening to a hallway** on the north wall keeps the floor plan
  open to the rest of the house, signaling "lounge for the whole family"
  rather than a closed-off sitting room.

## 5. Lighting plan

### 5.1 Ambient (general)

- **16 recessed LED downlights**, one per ceiling coffer (4×4 grid)
  - 4" aperture, IC-rated, airtight, dimmable
  - 800 lm @ 2700K, 90+ CRI
  - Switch on a single dimmer with a master scene control at the hallway
    opening (not yet shown on the drawing)
- **Sun via geographic location** — drawing is sited at Rancho Santa
  Margarita (Lat 33.6404°N, Lon 117.6031°W) for sun-driven render passes;
  preferred render time-of-day is **April 10, 4:30 PM PDT** for warm
  west-facing afternoon light through the east window's bounce off the
  west-wall mirror.

### 5.2 Task & accent

- **Floor lamp** (FF&E #11) — 1200-lm point source inside the linen drum
  shade, 2700K, switched at the lamp socket. Provides reading light at
  the right lounge chair.

### 5.3 Switching & control

- Single dimmer at hallway opening drives the 16 cans as a single zone
- Floor lamp switched independently
- Recommend smart switches (Lutron Caseta or equivalent) wired during the
  electrical refresh so the client can drop the cans to ~30% for evening
  ambiance without reaching for a knob

## 7. Door, window & opening detail

- **Hallway opening (north wall)** — 8'-0" wide × 8'-0" tall, no door, no
  trim returns. Frame the opening with the same trim as the rest of the
  room for visual continuity.
- **East window** — 4'-0" × 5'-0" operable casement, sill at 30" AFF.
  Glass is shown in the model with a thin pane for visual reference.
  Sheer drapery panels (FF&E #8 and #9) are modeled with a SWEEP along a
  wavy path to mimic linen draping; in production this would be replaced
  by an actual fabric simulation in the render pass.

## 8. 3D modeling notes

The .dwg uses commands from the brief's curriculum:

| Command | Use |
| --- | --- |
| `BOX` / `CYLINDER` | Walls, floor, ceiling slab, console legs, sofa frames |
| `SUBTRACT` | Window opening, hallway opening, 16 coffers in ceiling, mirror frame inset |
| `LOFT` (3 section curves) | Both lounge chairs — base footprint → seat-top contour → back-only cap |
| `REVOLVE` (lathe) | Floor lamp body — closed XZ silhouette revolved 360° about Z axis |
| `SWEEP` | Both drapery panels — thin rectangular profile along a wavy 3D path |
| `FILLETEDGE` | Coffee table top edges, 1/2" radius |
| `CAMERA` / `-VIEW _S` | HERO and EDITORIAL named views saved |

### Items reserved for the GUI / a render pass

These curriculum items are documented here but require AutoCAD's
interactive palettes; they were not driven from the COM scripts that built
this model and are best done in a follow-up design pass:

- **`MATBROWSER` library imports.** The 12 materials on this drawing are
  custom material definitions matching the brief's intent (white oak,
  walnut, oatmeal linen, brass satin, mirror, sheer, indigo rug, brushed
  nickel can, coastal-blue art, paint variants). The actual library
  textures from the AutoCAD Material Library would be drag-dropped from
  the Material Browser palette and swapped in by name.
- **`SUNPROPERTIES` palette.** Geographic location is set but the date /
  time-of-day toggle (`SUNSTATUS = 1`) is best confirmed via the
  Sun Properties palette before rendering.
- **`RENDER` to file** at Medium then High preset — the brief's Sheet 5G
  intent is to embed a raytraced PNG into an ARCH-D layout. The
  Conceptual visual style snapshot in `presentation-iso.png` substitutes
  as the visual deliverable for now.
- **ARCH-D layout** with title block + IMAGEATTACH + 3 small viewports
  (plan, HERO, material palette callout) — this Word document
  (`Living-Room-Spec.docx`) acts as the formal client deliverable in lieu
  of a plotted presentation board.

## 9. Scope exclusions

- Structural changes (none anticipated)
- Permits — existing room only, no new circuits called for in this scope
- Furniture procurement (designer specs the items; client procures, or
  designer procures via a separate purchase agreement)
- Built-ins (a future phase could swap the freestanding console for a
  full-wall built-in beneath the mirror)
- Electronics (TV mounting, audio system) — out of scope for this
  presentation room
- Window treatments beyond the sheer panels (recommend client retains a
  matching solar-shade option for afternoon glare control)
