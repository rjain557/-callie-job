# Company Intel — Mitchell Gold + Bob Williams (MG+BW)

## Snapshot
- **HQ:** Taylorsville, NC (manufacturing plant there)
- **Founded:** 1989 by Mitchell Gold and Bob Williams
- **Signature Stores:** 30+ nationwide, Costa Mesa in SoCal
- **What they sell:** Mid-to-upper-luxury furniture, design-forward, ethically made

## Why this is a great Callie target
1. **Design-led brand:** Founders were Calvin Klein furniture designers; brand is
   design credentialed
2. **Ethical manufacturing:** American-made in NC; clients buy for values + design
3. **Trade program:** Strong interior designer program
4. **Smaller footprint = less competition for hiring:** Fewer candidates apply
5. **Strong employer reputation:** Glassdoor scores consistently high

## Key positions to target
- **Design Consultant (Signature Store)**
- **Store Manager**
- **Trade Account Specialist**
- **Gallery Designer**

## Current climate (as of 2026)
- Chapter 11 filing in late 2024 created concern, but restructuring succeeded
- Now backed by Crescent Capital (PE firm)
- Showrooms reopening, growth-oriented again
- They're hiring proven design-sales people to rebuild

## Why the Chapter 11 history matters (and doesn't)
- If restructured successfully (as they did), this can be a GREAT time to join —
  new money, new strategy, need top performers
- Base salaries may be slightly lower but commission ceilings higher
- Interview process may be faster (they're motivated)

## Interview culture
- **Expect:** Phone screen → In-store interview → Final with District Manager
- **They value:** Strong communication skills, genuine design appreciation, ethical
  alignment, customer service excellence
- **Dress code:** Polished, contemporary, design-forward (they notice)
- **Culture fit signals:** Can you talk about design ethics (American-made, fair
  trade)? Do you recognize MG+BW's design aesthetic?

## Conversation hooks
- "Mitchell Gold's emphasis on domestic manufacturing and design values resonates
  with how I talked to clients at Ethan Allen"
- "I like that your pieces are investments — that's what I tried to do at EA, help
  clients buy furniture they'd love for 10 years, not 2"
- "The restructuring in 2024 showed the brand's resilience — I'd love to help it
  build back stronger"

## Compensation expectations
- Design Consultant: $45-55K base + commission
- Realistic year 1: $70-95K total
- Top performer: $100-130K
- Benefits: Standard retail (health, dental, 401k), now possibly leaner post-restructuring
- PTO: 2 weeks starting

## Negotiation intel
- Post-bankruptcy, they may be flexible on base but protective of commission structure
- Benefits may be thinner than pre-2024 but improving
- Growth opportunity is real — fast-track to management possible

## How to apply
**Ideal:** Find the Costa Mesa Signature Store Manager on LinkedIn, direct email
**Good:** mgbwhome.com/careers, then follow up personally
**Advantage:** Smaller recruiting team = faster response vs larger competitors

## Red flags
- If they push 100% commission — not MG+BW's historical model, be skeptical
- If interview process drags >3 weeks — organizational issue, may not be worth it
- If base offer is <$40K — below market

## Location near Callie
- Costa Mesa Signature Store (South Coast Plaza area) — ~25 mi from RSM
