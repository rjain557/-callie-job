# Company Intel — Restoration Hardware (RH)

## Snapshot
- **HQ:** Corte Madera, CA (but major design hub in Irvine/Newport Beach)
- **Public:** NYSE: RH
- **CEO:** Gary Friedman (since 2001, visionary/polarizing)
- **Revenue (FY2024):** ~$3B annually
- **Employees:** ~7,000
- **What they sell:** Premium residential furniture, lighting, textiles, hardware

## Why this is a great Callie target
1. **HQ design galleries** are in major residential markets with "Members of the Design Studio"
   programs — these are essentially design consultant roles at a luxury tier
2. **Compensation:** Base + commission structure for Design Studio members; strong
   performers make $100K+
3. **Brand alignment:** RH clients = Ethan Allen's top-tier clients, basically
4. **Growth:** Expanding to hospitality (RH Guesthouses) — more roles
5. **Gold Spirit Award is EXACTLY the kind of credential RH values**

## Key positions to target
- **Design Studio Associate** — entry into the brand, design + sales hybrid
- **Member of the Design Studio** — mid-level, higher commission
- **Gallery Leader / Co-Leader** — store management track (after 2-3 yrs)
- **Interior Design Associate** — specific design-heavy track

## Current climate (as of 2026)
- Recent earnings showed some margin pressure — they're cost-sensitive but still hiring
- Gary Friedman emphasizes "design is everything" culture — creative talent valued
- Recent push into hospitality (RH Guesthouse openings) means creative/service talent in demand
- They hire year-round in OC markets

## Interview culture
- **Expect:** Multiple rounds, one with Gary Friedman or senior leader (for higher roles)
- **They value:** Taste, discernment, polish, professionalism, ambition
- **They dislike:** Casualness, low-key dress, tentativeness
- **Dress code:** Showroom-appropriate luxury casual — clean, expensive-looking
- **Culture fit signals:** Can you speak about design with opinions? Do you know current
  design press (Architectural Digest, Elle Decor)?
- **Red flags to watch for from their side:** Long hours, high turnover, intense
  culture — they're direct about it in interviews

## Conversation hooks (use in email or interview)
- "Your expansion into hospitality with RH Guesthouse is the kind of design-led
  brand extension I admire"
- "The Design Studio model with consultative selling is what I did at Ethan Allen
  at tens of thousands of dollars smaller scale — I want to do it at RH scale"
- "[Specific current collection or designer collab]" — check rh.com before interview

## Compensation expectations
- Entry Design Studio Associate: $50-65K base + commission, realistic total $70-90K
- Member of Design Studio: $60-80K base + commission, realistic $90-140K
- Gallery Leader: $75-100K base + commission, realistic $110-160K

## Negotiation intel
- RH pays market rate — they're not above-market on base
- Commission structure is where top performers outpace peers
- They match health benefits typical of Fortune 1000 retail
- PTO is standard retail schedule (2 weeks to start)

## How to apply
**Preferred:** Reach out directly to Gallery Leader via LinkedIn
**Secondary:** Apply on rh.com/careers, then email the Gallery Leader directly to
signal interest ("I just applied for role X and wanted to reach out directly")
**Avoid:** Indeed/ZipRecruiter — their ATS routes through corporate and many get lost

## Red flags
- If they want her to "start as a sales associate and work up" — that's a volume role,
  not the Design Studio. Decline politely.
- If the offer doesn't include commission upside — it's the wrong role.
- If the Gallery Leader has been in role <6 months — instability signal, proceed with caution
