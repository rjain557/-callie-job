# Company Intel — Arhaus

## Snapshot
- **HQ:** Cleveland, OH
- **Founded:** 1986
- **Public:** NASDAQ: ARHS (IPO 2021)
- **Revenue:** ~$1.3B (FY2024)
- **Showrooms:** 80+ nationwide, 3-5 in Southern California
- **What they sell:** Luxury residential furniture, heirloom quality positioning

## Why this is a great Callie target
1. **Design trade program** — Arhaus Designers work with interior designers at 10-15%
   commission; this is Callie's existing relationship muscle
2. **Less corporate than RH** — family-founded, slightly more personal culture
3. **Growing fast** — IPO cash fuels showroom expansion, which means hiring
4. **Compensation upside** — strong performers clear $100K

## Key positions to target
- **Design Consultant** — showroom role with trade program tie-in
- **Gallery Designer** — design-heavy, commission-driven
- **Store Leader / Manager** (after 2-3 yrs)
- **Trade Account Specialist**

## Current climate (as of 2026)
- Post-IPO growth phase continues
- Emphasizes "artisanal" and "heirloom" messaging
- Less price pressure than RH/Pottery Barn segment
- Historically hires design-educated staff, not pure retail

## Interview culture
- **Expect:** Phone screen → In-store interview → Sometimes a second interview with
  District Manager
- **They value:** Interior design education or demonstrated knowledge, relationship
  skills, ability to discuss design trends
- **Dress code:** Elevated casual, approachable but polished
- **Culture fit signals:** Do you have opinions about design trends? Can you describe
  a current Arhaus collection you love?

## Conversation hooks
- "Your trade program is specifically what drew me — I've built warm relationships
  with interior designers over 5 years and know how to service that channel"
- "Arhaus' positioning around heirloom furniture matches how I sold at Ethan Allen
   — clients buying pieces they'd keep for decades, not trends they'd replace"
- "[Specific Arhaus collection] — I've had clients ask about pieces like that"

## Compensation expectations
- Design Consultant: $45-60K base + commission
- Realistic year 1 total: $70-95K
- Top performer year 2+: $100-130K
- Benefits: Standard retail (health, 401k match, employee discount)

## Negotiation intel
- Arhaus base tends to be lower than RH, but commission structure is more accessible
- Employee discount is very good (design staff often furnish their own homes at cost)
- Training investment is serious — 4-6 week ramp
- 2 weeks PTO starting

## How to apply
**Ideal:** Direct email to Gallery Leader at target location
**Good:** arhaus.com/careers, then personal follow-up via LinkedIn
**Backup:** Through Indeed (they do hire from there, but slower)

## Red flags
- If they want "2 years retail management" as strict requirement — move to different role
- If commission capped at low level — less upside than competitors
- Closing rate expectations — if they quote <20% closure, it's a volume-hungry role

## Locations near Callie
- Costa Mesa showroom (South Coast Plaza) — 25 mi from RSM
- Fashion Island Newport Beach — ~20 mi from RSM
- Check if Laguna Hills has opened — they've been expanding
