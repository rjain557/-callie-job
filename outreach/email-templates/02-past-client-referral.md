# Template 2 — Past Client Referral Ask

## When to use
For past Ethan Allen clients you worked with over 3+ touchpoints (they trust you).
These people are your warmest leads — they already know your work and likely have
networks in the interior design / home improvement world.

## Subject line options
- `Quick update — and a small favor`
- `It's been a while — exciting news`
- `[Client name] — quick question`

## Email body

```
Hi [Client First Name],

I hope [your recent project / your home / your summer] has been going well. I was
thinking about the [SPECIFIC project you did together — "kitchen refresh" / "living
room refresh" / "master bedroom"] and smiled remembering how [ONE SPECIFIC thing that
worked out — "the fabric choice really did stand the test of time" / "the pieces all
came together beautifully"].

I wanted to reach out with some news — after an amazing three years at Ethan Allen
where I earned the Gold Spirit Award, I'm exploring my next move in residential
design. I'm looking for a role where I can keep doing what I love most: helping people
make their homes beautiful and functional.

If you happen to know anyone — a designer, a showroom, a design-build firm, a contractor
— who might be hiring or know someone who is, I'd be so grateful for an introduction.
A quick forward of my resume would mean a lot.

Thank you for being such a wonderful client. I'd love to hear how life has been.

Warm regards,
Callie
949-557-9014
```

## Rules
- **Reference the SPECIFIC project** — proves you remember them as a person, not a transaction
- **Warmth before the ask** — lead with the personal
- **Small, specific ask** — "if you know anyone, forward my resume" (not "help me find a job")
- **Don't attach resume** — just mention forwarding
- **Send one at a time** — personalize each one; batch-and-blast feels cheap
- **Target 5 clients the first week** — space them out, don't spam

## Who to send this to (criteria)
- 3+ interactions with you (full project engagement, not one-off)
- Positive working relationship (they came back for more or referred friends)
- Lives/works in a professional circle likely to have design industry connections
- Ideally: business owner, real estate agent, contractor, designer, or affluent homeowner

## Who NOT to send to
- Single-visit clients
- Clients with any complaint history
- Former colleagues at Ethan Allen (use Template 3 for them)

## Tracking
Log outreach in `outreach/tracking.md` — date sent, name, response, notes.
