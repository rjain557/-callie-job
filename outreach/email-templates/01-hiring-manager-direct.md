# Template 1 — Direct Hiring Manager Outreach

## When to use
When you've identified a specific hiring manager, Design Director, or VP of Sales at a company — and want to bypass the ATS/HR black hole. Sent DIRECTLY to their work email (not a jobs@ inbox).

## Subject line options (pick one)
- `Design sales candidate — $800K at Ethan Allen — introduction`
- `Former Ethan Allen Design Consultant — quick intro`
- `[Mutual connection name] suggested I reach out` (ONLY if true)

## Email body (personalize bracketed sections)

```
Hi [First Name],

I'm reaching out directly because I noticed [SPECIFIC thing about the company — recent
expansion / new showroom / project you admire / award they won]. I spent the past three
years as a Design Consultant at Ethan Allen, where I generated over $800,000 in design
sales and earned the 2025 Gold Spirit Award for client relationships and revenue
performance.

[One-sentence why this company specifically — "Your Laguna showroom's focus on luxury
residential matches exactly the clientele I worked with" or "Your consultative sales
model is what I thrived in at Ethan Allen"].

I'd love to find 20 minutes for a quick conversation about whether my background could
serve [Company Name]. I'm available [day of week] or [another day] — does either work?

Either way, thanks for reading.

Callie Nichole Wells
949-557-9014
linkedin.com/in/callie-wells17
```

## Rules
- **Under 150 words** — hiring managers skim
- **Lead with numbers** ($800K, Gold Spirit Award) — those are objective credentials
- **One specific thing about their company** — shows you did homework
- **One clear ask** (20-minute conversation)
- **NEVER attach resume unsolicited** — let them ask
- **NEVER use "I'm passionate about"** or "I think I'd be a great fit" — cliché
- Send 9-11am on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (best open rates)

## Follow-up if no response
After 5 business days, reply to your own email:

```
Hi [First Name], following up in case my note got buried. Still very interested in
connecting if there's a fit. Happy to send resume if useful. Thanks, Callie
```
