Good recruiters are free to you (employers pay them), they know the inside of
companies you can't see from job boards, and a great one can get you in front of 5-10
hiring managers in a week. Worth engaging 3-5 of them.

## How to work with recruiters (rules)

**DO:**
- Reach out to 3-5 in parallel — recruiters expect this
- Be clear about what you're looking for (role, comp, location)
- Respond fast when they do send you something
- Send thank-you notes after they intro you

**DON'T:**
- Ghost them after they do work for you
- Negotiate around them directly with the employer (burns bridges)
- Apply to the same role through them AND directly to the company (recruiters lose the placement fee)

Location: Rancho Santa Margarita, CA — commuting up to 50 miles
Remote: Open to remote roles for W-2 positions
Target comp: $65K+ base for admin, $75K+ base for design (higher for commission)

Would love 15 minutes if any of your current searches might fit. Happy to send a
resume for your files either way.

Thanks,
Callie Wells
949-557-9014
linkedin.com/in/callie-wells17

## Expected response times
- Top-tier recruiters: 2-3 business days
- High-volume recruiters (Robert Half, etc.): 1-2 business days (auto-triage)
- Specialty firms: 1 week+ (worth the wait)
